Dongfang Xu

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST). In Dec. 2022, I received my Ph.D. degree (mit Auszeichnung bestanden) in electrical engineering from Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU), Erlangen, Germany, under the supervision of Prof. Robert Schober. Previously, I received my M.Sc. degree (mit Auszeichnung bestanden) in communications and multimedia engineering from FAU in Sept. 2017 and my B.Eng. degree in communication engineering from Shandong University, Jinan, China, in Jul. 2014, respectively.

    Research Interests

    Here, I would like to list some interesting topics for potential future research. Also, I would like to summarize some exciting optimization methods and tricks refined from my works.

  • Heterogeneous 6G Network

    • I am quite interested in investigating the resource allocation design for highly heterogeneous 6G network including unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs)-assisted communications, intelligent reflecting surfaces (IRSs)-empowered communications, high-speed railway wireless communications, vehicular communications, and integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), etc.

  • Optimization Tricks in Wireless Communications

    1. Disjunctive programming and mixed-integer linear programming

      2. By exploiting disjunctive programming (DP), one can manipulate a set of linear inequalities to model vehicle collisions or obstacle avoidances, e.g., to ensure UAV sidestep practical polygon no-fly zone. In particular, the coordinate of the position should satisfy at least one of the linear inequalities, leading to a disjunctive logic constraint. Subsequently, the DP problem can be equivalently transformed into a mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) problem which can be tackled by many popular approaches.
